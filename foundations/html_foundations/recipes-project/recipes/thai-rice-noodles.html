Thai-style seafood with rice noodles

Little description from me.

This is a recipe in the internet. But it seems to be pretty quick and easy to make. Not so healthy, according to the recipe publisher, but anyway better and tastier than fast-cooked noodles, made out of paper(in the best case).

Ingredients:

  • Thick rice noodles, of course.
  • Whatever oil you have for frying stuff. The recipe owner advises with "canola oil", but who cares, I'll use olive oil, works just fine, too.
  • Curry paste. Yeah, it's getting interesting.
  • Can coconut cream.
  • Fish sauce.
  • Lime/lemon juice.
  • Seafood mix.
  • Red capsicum.
  • Onions.

Well, I have no idea how I'm gonna cook it, because most of this ingredients are impossible to buy at my place. But, as always, who cares. I'll cook it anyway.

How to cook it.

  1. Place noodles in a heatproof bowl. Cover with boiling water. Stand for 1 to 2 minutes or until tender. Using a fork, separate noodles. Drain. Rinse under cold water.
  2. Spray a saucepan with oil. Heat over medium-high heat. Add curry paste. Cook, stirring, for 1 minute or until fragrant. Add coconut cream, fish sauce and lime juice. Bring to the boil. Reduce heat to medium. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Add seafood, capsicum and onions. Simmer, covered, for 4 to 5 minutes or until seafood is cooked through. Add noodles. Toss to combine. Cook for 1 to 2 minutes or until heated through. EAT.
